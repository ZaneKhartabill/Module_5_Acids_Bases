SMALLER ENERGY GAP = STRONGER ACID/BASE

Acid and base strength depends on the energy gap between reactants and products. This fundamental principle guides our understanding of acid-base behavior.

2 Finding the Most Basic Site

Question:

Which atom is most basic in the following molecule?

Solution:
  1. Identify potential basic sites:
    • Pyridine nitrogen (N1)
    • Pyrrolidine nitrogen (N2)
  2. Apply the 5 factors:
    • Size: Both are nitrogen atoms, so they have the same size. This factor doesn't help us differentiate.
    • Electronegativity: Both are nitrogen atoms with identical electronegativity.
    • Inductive effects: The pyrrolidine N2 has electron-donating alkyl groups (EDG's - two CH2 alkyl groups = lone pair even more "available" for bonding)
    • Hybridization: Pyridine N is sp¬≤ (33% s), pyrrolidine N is sp¬≥ (25% s)
    • Resonance: Pyridine N lone pair participates in aromatic resonance
  3. Conclusion: The pyrrolidine nitrogen (N2) is more basic because:
    • Its lone pair is not part of aromatic resonance (more available)
    • It has less s-character (sp¬≥ vs sp¬≤)
    • Approximate pKa values: Pyrrolidine (conjugate acid pKa ‚âà 11) vs Pyridine (conjugate acid pKa ‚âà 5)
